## Tuong Do

Tuong Do writes about the people history tried to bury — the women, the enslaved, the translators, the outcasts, and the border-dwellers who shaped the world from its margins. Every major mystery in his fiction is real and unresolved; the stories live in the gaps of the historical record.

With over 30 years of experience building global teams across four continents and six countries, he brings a perspective shaped by crossing borders — cultural, linguistic, and professional — to narratives that span civilizations, oceans, and centuries.

His career has taken him from writing GIS code as an intern in 1996 to leading global data and AI organizations, including scaling Hershey's data team and leading AI strategy at Wells Fargo. He holds a dual MBA from Purdue's Krannert School of Management and TiasNimbas Business School.

### Fiction

**The Nomenclator Chronicles** — a five-book series following a cipher manuscript across 225 years of pirate history, from the slave markets of Algiers (1585) to Ching Shih's fleet in the South China Sea (1810). All five books are complete.

**The Thousand Li of Shadows** — a five-book series spanning sixteen centuries of Silk Road history, connected by a jade-bronze merchant's seal passed from hand to hand across the ages. In progress.

### Nonfiction

**[Catching Lightning in a Bottle]({{ site.author_info.website }})** — a framework for building exceptional distributed teams in the AI era, drawing on 30+ years of experience across startups, government, Fortune 500, and his own ventures.
