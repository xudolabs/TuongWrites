## Tuong Do

I write about the people history tried to bury — the women, the enslaved, the translators, the outcasts, and the border-dwellers who shaped the world from its margins.

Every major mystery in my fiction is real and unresolved. The stories live in the gaps of the historical record — the places where documents end, where civilizations were erased, where someone sealed a library behind a false wall and no one knows why.

I spent 30 years crossing borders — cultural, linguistic, professional — building teams across four continents and six countries. That experience taught me something that shapes every book I write: the most important stories happen at the edges, where worlds collide and the people in between must choose who they are.

## The Nomenclator Chronicles

A five-book series following a cipher manuscript across 225 years of pirate history. From the slave markets of Algiers in 1585 to Ching Shih's fleet in the South China Sea in 1810, each book follows a different protagonist who discovers the Nomenclator, decodes fragments from previous eras, and adds their own testimony. The series asks one question across two centuries: *Can freedom exist outside the law?* All five books are complete.

## The Thousand Li of Shadows

A five-book series spanning sixteen centuries of Silk Road history, from 97 CE to the fall of Constantinople in 1453. Connected by a jade-and-bronze merchant's seal passed from hand to hand across the ages, the series unearths the untold mysteries of the ancient trade routes — lost cities swallowed by desert, letters that never arrived, libraries sealed for nine centuries, civilizations erased from memory. Currently in progress.
