In 1338, in a Nestorian Christian community near Lake Issyk-Kul in Kyrgyzstan, people begin to die. Tombstones carved that year specifically mention pestilence. Modern DNA analysis has confirmed: this is ground zero for the Black Death. Within a decade, the plague will kill a third of Europe, devastate the Middle East, and effectively end the Silk Road.

The book follows the plague along the trade routes — from Issyk-Kul to the Siege of Caffa, where infected corpses may have been catapulted over the walls in history's first biological warfare, to Constantinople, to Cairo, to the maritime routes. But this is not just a plague story. It is the story of the end of an era.

What dies when a road dies? Not just trade — but the flow of ideas, the mixing of cultures, the possibility that a Buddhist monk in Beijing could walk to Rome.

## Historical Backdrop

The Issyk-Kul tombstones (confirmed by 2022 DNA analysis), the Siege of Caffa, Tamerlane's conquests, Zheng He's treasure fleet voyages, the massacre of foreign merchants in Quanzhou, and the fall of Constantinople in 1453.

## Series Thread

*The Last Courier carries the jade-bronze seal one final time* — from Samarkand to Constantinople as the routes close behind him. The seal completes its 1,356-year journey. The road is gone, but nothing it carried can be unlearned.
