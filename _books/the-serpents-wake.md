Adaeze, a formerly enslaved woman freed when pirates captured her transport ship, washes ashore on Madagascar — the legendary pirate haven where rumor says free people built a utopia called Libertalia. Armed with the Nomenclator and a treasure map, she journeys into the island's interior to find whether noble dreams matter even when they fail.

A treasure hunt through uncharted jungle, a siege at sea, and the question at the heart of the series: can freedom survive its own contradictions?

## Historical Backdrop

Set in the twilight of Indian Ocean piracy, the novel explores the real legends of Libertalia, the pirate settlements of Île Sainte-Marie, Ratsimilaho's Betsimisaraka kingdom, and the contested history of pirate utopias in Madagascar.

## Series Thread

*The question becomes mythological: Did utopia ever exist?*

Adaeze adds testimony about whether noble dreams matter even if they fail. The Nomenclator is entrusted to a Chinese trader in the Madagascar–Pearl River network, connecting to the series finale.
