A disgraced Portuguese naval officer is captured by the fleet of Ching Shih — the most successful pirate in history, commanding 800 junks and 25,000 people across the South China Sea. As he decodes the Nomenclator's 224 years of testimony, he realizes her confederation is everything the previous writers dreamed of. He must decide whether to record the truth or protect her secret.

The series finale answers the question the Nomenclator has been asking for two centuries: *"Can freedom exist outside the law?"* The answer is yes — but at what cost?

## Historical Backdrop

Ching Shih's Red Flag Fleet was the largest pirate confederation in history. Her legal code — including the death penalty for rape and equal share of plunder — created a floating nation. The novel follows the real negotiations between her fleet, the Qing Dynasty, the Portuguese, and the British.
