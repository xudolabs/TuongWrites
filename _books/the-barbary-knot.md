In June 1585, off the coast of Sardinia, Sir Domenico Zammit's galley is captured by Ottoman corsairs under the legendary Uluç Ali Reis — an elderly admiral commanding from his flagship. Chained as a galley slave and transported to Algiers, Domenico enters the beylik bagnio, the sprawling Christian slave prison where thousands of captives navigate a brutal ransom economy.

In the bagnio, Domenico encounters Thomas Ashford, an English apostate shipwright who has converted to Islam and designs innovative corsair galleys, earning honor in Ottoman society. When the Sephardic merchant Mateo Benveniste offers to pay Domenico's full ransom — 3,000 ducats plus 2,000 in gold — in exchange for extracting Thomas Ashford and delivering him to Venice within one year, Domenico is drawn into a web of espionage, commerce, and competing loyalties that stretches across the entire Mediterranean.

Caught between the warring faiths of the age — Christian knights, Ottoman corsairs, Sephardic merchants, and the shadowy Syndicate of Crowns — Domenico discovers the Nomenclator in an Ottoman archive. The leather-bound cipher manuscript contains older encrypted entries written by multiple hands, and he must decode its fragments while navigating a world where the line between "civilization" and "piracy" is drawn by whoever holds the sword.

## Historical Backdrop

Set against the great Mediterranean struggle between the Ottoman Empire and Christian Europe in the decades following the Battle of Lepanto. The novel features historical figures including:

- **Uluç Ali Reis** — the legendary corsair admiral, aged approximately 80, commanding the Ottoman fleet
- **Miguel de Cervantes** — the future author of *Don Quixote*, reimagined as a fellow captive in Algiers
- **Mami Corso** — Barbary corsair captain
- **Trinitarian ransoming monks** — the religious order that negotiated captives' freedom

The novel portrays with vivid accuracy the Mediterranean slave trade, the ransom economy of Algiers, the multilingual environment where Arabic, Turkish, Spanish, Italian, Lingua Franca, and Hebrew collided daily, and the complex social hierarchies of Ottoman North Africa.

## Series Thread

*The question is born: Can freedom exist outside the law?*

Domenico discovers the Nomenclator in an Ottoman archive — it contains older entries from previous eras. He adds his own testimony about the blurred lines between "civilization" and "piracy." Exiled after his mission, Domenico ends up in Jamaica among early buccaneers, where he dies in 1627. He bequeaths the Nomenclator to a Maroon community, where it waits nearly half a century for the next protagonist.
