A Maltese Knight of St. John is captured during a galley battle and enslaved in Algiers. Caught between the warring faiths of the Mediterranean — Christian knights, Ottoman corsairs, and the shadowy Syndicate of Crowns — he discovers the Nomenclator in an Ottoman archive and must navigate a world where the line between "civilization" and "piracy" is drawn by whoever holds the sword.

Navigating the blurred lines between faith, piracy, and survival, Domenico must decode the manuscript's secrets while brokering his own freedom in a world where captivity and liberty are matters of perspective.

## Historical Backdrop

Set against the great Mediterranean struggle between the Ottoman Empire and Christian Europe, featuring historical figures including Uluç Ali Reis — the legendary corsair admiral — and Miguel de Cervantes as a fellow captive in Algiers.

## Series Thread

*The question is born: Can freedom exist outside the law?*

Domenico discovers the Nomenclator in an Ottoman archive. It contains older entries. He adds his own testimony about the blurred lines between "civilization" and "piracy," then bequeaths it to a Maroon community in Jamaica, where it waits for the next protagonist.
