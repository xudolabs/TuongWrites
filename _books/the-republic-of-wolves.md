A young idealistic scribe arrives in Nassau to document the world's only pirate republic — a place where every man votes, plunder is shared equally, and 25–30% of crews are African or mixed-race men fighting as equals. But as Woodes Rogers sails to crush this experiment in democracy, Thomas must choose between recording history and making it.

Featuring Blackbeard, Charles Vane, Anne Bonny, and Calico Jack Rackham, *The Republic of Wolves* asks whether pirates truly built a democracy — and why the world couldn't let it stand.

## Historical Backdrop

The golden age of piracy's most explosive chapter: the Flying Gang, the pirate council at Nassau, the King's Pardon, Woodes Rogers' arrival, Blackbeard's last stand, and Vane's execution. Every major event in the novel is historically documented.

## Series Thread

*The question becomes political: Can pirates govern themselves?*

Thomas documents the rise and fall of the republic — democracy's brief, imperfect bloom. He escapes Nassau's fall and flees to Madagascar, where he hands the Nomenclator directly to Book 4's protagonist.
