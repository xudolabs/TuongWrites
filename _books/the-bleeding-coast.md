A Spanish deserter joins Henry Morgan's notorious expedition against Panama, secretly searching for his kidnapped sister María. As he marches through nine days of jungle with a thousand buccaneers, he discovers how revolutionary movements get co-opted by empire-builders — and that the Nomenclator carries warnings from a century before.

From the forts of Porto Bello to the burning streets of Panama City, *The Bleeding Coast* is a story of brotherhood, betrayal, and the terrible cost of empire dressed as liberation.

## Historical Backdrop

The novel unfolds during Henry Morgan's real 1670–1671 campaign — the assault on Porto Bello's three forts, the siege of San Lorenzo, and the nine-day jungle march to Panama City. Historical figures include Morgan himself and Governor Sir Thomas Modyford.

## Series Thread

*The question is weaponized: freedom as empire's tool.*

Alejandro adds a warning to the Nomenclator about how revolutionary movements get co-opted by empire-builders. It passes to Anne, a tavern-keeper in Port Royal who runs an intelligence network. Her successor carries it to Nassau.
