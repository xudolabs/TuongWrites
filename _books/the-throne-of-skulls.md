Three converging storylines across the Mongol era:

**The Destruction.** A Tangut scholar in Khara-Khoto races to preserve his civilization's literature as Genghis Khan's armies close in. The Tangut script — 6,000 characters created to rival Chinese — will become unreadable for 700 years.

**The Library of the Assassins.** A young Ismaili woman at the fortress of Alamut guards the library of Hassan-i Sabbah. When Hulagu Khan's Mongol army besieges the fortress, she must choose what to save and what to let burn.

**The Reverse Marco Polo.** Rabban Bar Sauma, a Nestorian Christian monk born in Beijing, travels westward across the entire Mongol Empire to meet the Pope, the King of France, and the King of England. He celebrates the Eucharist in Rome. Almost no one has heard of him.

## Historical Backdrop

The Mongol destruction of Khwarezmia, the erasure of the Tangut civilization, Alamut's legendary library, the sack of Baghdad, and the Pax Mongolica. Also featuring Guillaume Boucher, a Parisian goldsmith enslaved by the Mongols who builds wonders at Karakorum.

## Series Thread

*The jade-bronze seal is recovered by Sonam from Khara-Khoto* and carried through Mongol territory, surviving the most violent century in Silk Road history.
